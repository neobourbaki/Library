Let $M$ be a module over a ring $R$, and $N$ be a submodule of $M$. Then the *quotient module* $M/N$ is the equivalence classes of $m \in M$, under the relation $m \sim m'$ if $m - m' \in N$. The equivalence class of $m$ is the set $m + N$. The addition and multiplication by elements of $R$ are defined by

- $(m + N) + (m' + N) = (m + m' + N)$
- $r(m + N) = (rm + N)$

making $M/N$ a $R$-module.
